Question: Explain Working Principle of Transformer?

Transformer Working Principle:

A transformer operates on the principle of **mutual induction**, transferring electrical energy between circuits while changing voltage levels, as illustrated in the diagram below.

Figure: Basic Construction and Principle of Transformer

A transformer is an electrical device that transfers electrical energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction. The fundamental principle behind its operation is mutual induction, which occurs when a changing magnetic field in one winding induces an EMF in another winding.

Here’s how it works:

  • **Primary Winding:** When an alternating current (AC) flows through the primary winding, it generates a changing magnetic field around it. This magnetic field creates a magnetic flux that passes through the core of the transformer.
  • **Core:** The core is typically made of **laminated silicon steel**, which provides a **low reluctance path** for the magnetic flux, enhancing the efficiency of the transformer by minimizing energy losses due to eddy currents.
  • **Secondary Winding:** The changing magnetic flux in the core links with the secondary winding. According to **Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction**, this changing flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in the secondary winding.
  • **Voltage Transformation:** The voltage induced in the secondary winding depends on the **turns ratio** between the primary ($N_1$) and secondary ($N_2$) windings. If the secondary winding has more turns ($N_2 > N_1$), the transformer is a **step-up transformer**, increasing the voltage. Conversely, if the primary has more turns ($N_1 > N_2$), it is a **step-down transformer**, decreasing the voltage.
