2.a) Explain the Working Principle of a Transformer?

Transformer Working Principle:

A transformer is an electrical device that transfers electrical energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction. The fundamental principle behind its operation is mutual induction, which occurs when a changing magnetic field in one winding induces an EMF in another winding.

Here’s how it works:

  • Primary Winding: When an Alternating Current (AC) flows through the primary winding, it generates a changing magnetic field around it. This magnetic field creates a magnetic flux ($\Phi$) that passes through the core of the transformer.
  • Core: The core is typically made of laminated silicon steel, which provides a low reluctance path for the magnetic flux, enhancing the efficiency of the transformer.
  • Secondary Winding: The changing magnetic flux ($\Phi$) in the core links with the secondary winding. According to Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction, this changing flux induces an electromotive force (EMF) in the secondary winding.
  • Voltage Transformation: The voltage induced in the secondary winding depends on the turns ratio ($N_S/N_P$). If $N_S > N_P$, it is a step-up transformer (voltage increases). If $N_S < N_P$, it is a step-down transformer (voltage decreases).

1.a) What is an ideal voltage source?

An ideal voltage source is a two-terminal source whose voltage remains constant regardless of the current drawn from its terminals.

It has zero internal resistance ($R_i = 0$).

1.b) Define KVL (Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law).

Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL) states that the algebraic sum of all voltages (drops and rises) around any closed loop in a circuit must be zero.

This law is based on the law of conservation of energy.

1.c) What is active power?

Active power ($P$) is the actual power consumed by the load to perform useful work.

It is measured in Watts (W) and represents the real energy transfer in a circuit.

1.d) Define the average value of a sinusoidal quantity.

The average value of a sinusoidal quantity over a half-cycle is the arithmetic mean of all instantaneous values over that period.

For a full cycle, the average value is zero.

1.e) What is a step-up transformer?

A step-up transformer increases the input voltage from the primary winding to a higher output voltage at the secondary winding.

It achieves this by having **fewer turns on the primary** than on the secondary winding.

1.f) Define the regulation of a transformer.

Regulation is defined as the change in the secondary terminal voltage from no-load to full-load.

It is expressed as a percentage of the full-load voltage and indicates the transformer’s ability to maintain a constant output voltage.

1.g) What type of material is used for the brushes of a d.c machine?

Carbon or Graphite materials are primarily used for brushes in a d.c. machine.

1.h) How many windings are present at the time of starting a single-phase Induction motor?

A single-phase induction motor has **two windings** present at the time of starting:

  • The main (running) winding
  • The starting (auxiliary) winding
1.i) What is the full form of M.C.C.B.?

The full form of M.C.C.B. is Moulded Case Circuit Breaker.

1.j) What is the energy consumed by a 1000 watts heater in 3 hours?

The energy consumed is 3 kWh (kilowatt-hours).

Calculation: $\text{Energy} = \text{Power} \times \text{Time}$

$$1000 \, \text{W} \times 3 \, \text{h} = 3000 \, \text{Wh} = 3 \, \text{kWh}$$
